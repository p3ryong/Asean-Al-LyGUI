The translation of Uten Mahamid's poem "Three Line Poem" by Mui Poopoksakul offers a thought-provoking backdrop for contemplation. The Ungrateful Man

The narrative of "The Ungrateful Man" offers a provocative examination of subjects including appreciation, generosity, betrayal, and justice. Its core message is that kindness breeds compassion, whereas ungratefulness and betrayal can have negative effects of their own. The hunter's initial deed of generosity in rescuing the man, the rat, the serpent, and the leopard from the pit serves as evidence of his intrinsic goodness. Acting out of compassion, he demonstrates his regard for life and willingness to lend a hand despite personal grudges, as he did with the leopard who had injured him in the past. The story uses this initial act of kindness to show the value of generosity even when it is not anticipated or returned. The animals' subsequent displays of gratitude, especially the serpent's gift of a priceless remedy and the leopard's provision of food, serve as an illustration of the belief that deeds of kindness have a positive snowball impact on other people's attitudes. These acts serve as a reminder that generosity and kindness, no matter where it comes from, can forge relationships of gratitude and yield unexpected benefits. On the other hand, the ungrateful man's personality stands out sharply. He betrays the hunter who saved his life out of jealousy and self-interest. This betrayal ultimately results in his own destruction when he is revealed to be the real traitor when he attempts to falsely accuse the hunter of stealing. The narrative shows how ungratefulness and disloyalty frequently result in undesirable outcomes and ultimately compromise one's integrity. As a result of the hunter's ability to heal the King's son with the serpent's antidote and bring about justice, the ungrateful man is brought to ruin. This highlights the notion that justice frequently wins out in the end, rewarding virtue and punishing wickedness. In conclusion, "The Ungrateful Man" is a fable-like story that functions as a moral parable, teaching us about the transformational power of kindness and the repercussions of ingratitude and betrayal. It serves as a reminder that goodness has the power to bring about wonderful things, whereas dishonesty and ungraciousness can result in one's own destruction. This story is a timeless allegory about human nature and the virtue of upholding one's moral principles.
